⚕
Why no running in Meso 1: Active left ITB/knee pain + quad dominance + neurologically inhibited left glute med = running loads will further entrench the compensation pattern and increase tissue load. The body will run with TFL and quad dominant, not glute dominant. We must establish correct neuromuscular patterns in controlled environments before adding the complexity and speed of running gait. Uphill walking is ideal: higher glute demand, low impact, no lateral knee shear.
Cardio Prescription — Meso 1
Primary — Uphill Walking

Incline 8-15% · RPE 4-5

Treadmill incline walking. Start at 8%, build to 15% by week 3. 30-50 min sessions. Drives posterior chain demand without knee shear. Excellent glute activation under load. 3x/week.

Secondary — Cross Trainer

Elliptical · RPE 4-5

Low impact, full ROM, no lateral knee force. 30-40 min steady state or intervals. Good aerobic base builder. Cue: push through heel — don't pull with quads. 2x/week max.

Optional — Assault Bike

Monitor Knee Response

Only if lateral knee pain does not increase during or 24hrs after session. Upper body dominant drive initially. Assess after first session. If knee pain VAS increases: remove from programme and use cross trainer instead.

Excluded — Running

No Running Meso 1

Running reintroduced in Meso 2 only if gate criteria are met at week 4 retest. Any running in Meso 1 risks entrenching quad/TFL dominance pattern further and aggravating ITB irritation.

Nutrition — Full Programme
⚙
Dual Goal Strategy: 73 kg → 65-70 kg AND sub-4:00 marathon. These are compatible with phased approach: caloric deficit P1-P2 when intensity is low, transition to maintenance from Meso 3 when mileage demands it. Never deficit in peak mileage weeks. Performance before aesthetics from week 9 onwards.
Phased Caloric Targets
Meso 1-2 · Weeks 1-8

Moderate Deficit

~1,900-2,100 kcal/day. Deficit -250-300 kcal. Cardio energy expenditure is lower (walking vs running) so deficit is sustainable. Target 0.2-0.3 kg/week loss.

Meso 3 · Weeks 9-13

Maintenance

~2,300-2,500 kcal. Weight may plateau — this is correct. Running mileage increases energy demand significantly. Trying to deficit at 50+ km/week risks injury and hormonal disruption.

Meso 4 · Weeks 14-17

Performance +100

~2,400-2,600 kcal. Small surplus. Highest mileage in programme. Fuel the adaptation. Weight is irrelevant during peak. Performance is everything.

Meso 5 · Weeks 18-20

Taper + Carb Load

Wk 18-19: maintenance. Race week: structured carb load. Day before race: 8-10g carb/kg (~520-650g). Race morning: 2-3g carb/kg. Race-day nutrition is performance nutrition.

Protein Priority — All Phases
Target

1.8-2.2g/kg bodyweight

~131-161g/day at 73kg. Distribute across 4-5 meals. High protein preserves muscle during caloric deficit and supports tissue repair — critical given TFL/ITB injury history.

Timing

Post-Session Priority

30-40g protein within 45 min of any session >60 min. Pre-bed protein (casein/Greek yogurt) for overnight muscle protein synthesis. Every meal should contain a protein source.

Iron

Female Runner Priority

High mileage increases iron demand. Red meat 2x/wk or lentils/spinach daily. Vitamin C with non-haem iron sources. Ferritin blood test recommended week 8. Low ferritin = fatigue and poor adaptation.

Hydration

2.5-3.5L/day

Pale yellow urine = adequate. Add 500ml per hour of training. Electrolyte tab post any session >60 min. Salt food to taste during high mileage weeks. Tart cherry juice before bed — supports recovery.

If Lateral Knee Flares During Race

Immediate Response

Shorten stride. Increase cadence to 175+. Do not compensate with hip hike — this will worsen TFL load. Lean forward slightly from the hip.

If Pain Reaches 5+/10

Brief stop. 30-60 sec TFL stretch. Reassess. Is it getting worse with running or manageable? Make the call: finish smart or protect long-term health.
